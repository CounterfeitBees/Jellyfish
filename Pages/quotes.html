Naive Ontological Realism - Ethan Ludwin-Peery
An ontology is how you look at the world. For example, looking at a door and knowing you can go through it requires an ontology. The ontology is "doors can be opened and closed and I can walk through them." But it also carries some other related ontologies such as "windows are not for entering buildings" or "buildings are to be entered through walls, not ceilings."
Ontological Realism is the belief that there is one true, real, or correct ontology. It tends to come with the acompanying belief that everyone should know the ontology and often creates discomfort in induviduals who must watch the people who do not have the truth.
Naive Ontological Realism is when one doesn't even know that you don't have to be an Ontological Realist. Naive Ontological Realists aren't open to changing thier minds about what is true real or correct because they do not beleive or understand how that could be done.
